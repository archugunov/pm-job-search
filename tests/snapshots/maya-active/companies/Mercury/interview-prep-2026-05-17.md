## Pricing POV — the gap from the case study

Coming in with a hypothesis this time:

> Per-entity SaaS will read as the cleaner mental model for bookkeeping firms because the firm already prices their own service per-client-entity. Friction comes when a firm onboards a one-off client they want to test — per-entity creates psychological "is this worth the £X" friction. Compromise: per-entity SaaS with the first 3 entities free per firm. That preserves the headline pricing simplicity but kills the test-client friction.

Will hedge it as "my working hypothesis, want to stress-test it with you" — Immad's response will tell me whether the pricing is genuinely open or already decided.

## Founder-vetting questions (the most important part)

1. **The 18-month-downside question:** "If Mercury's Banking strategy in 2028 looks like a miss, what's the most likely shape of the miss?" Tests whether Immad has named the failure modes or just the success path.

2. **The political-capital question:** "Where has the previous Banking PM lead spent most political capital internally?" Looking for specificity — vague answers signal a founder who hasn't watched closely.

3. **The decision-loop question:** "When the Banking team ships something that underperforms — who's in the debrief, and who actually changes their behaviour as a result?" Tests whether failure is a learning event or a blame event at Mercury.

4. **If time:** "What's the version of this role you'd love to write the offer letter for in two years — what would they have built that the current org couldn't?" Tests whether Immad has growth-path thinking for the role or is just hiring for present scope.
