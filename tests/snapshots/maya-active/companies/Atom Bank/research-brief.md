## Company snapshot

- **Stage:** ~450 ppl, app-only UK challenger bank, Durham-based.
- **What they sell:** Retail bank — savings, mortgages, business loans through the app.
- **The role:** Head of Product with 8+ direct reports. GM-shaped in practice — multi-team management, budget ownership, cross-functional coordination.

## Why this fits

**P2 — closed pre-application. Two anti-goal violations.**

- **Size:** 450 ppl is three times my preferred ceiling.
- **Role-shape:** 8+ direct reports is the GM job I explicitly don't want. I want to be close to the product and close to the founders, not managing managers.
- **Pattern note:** At bank-scale shops, HoP titles tend to resolve into people-management with budget ownership as the primary work. Worth capturing for the next time this shape appears.
- **Verdict:** When size and scope both fail the anti-goal check, the right move is to close immediately rather than spend cycles on due diligence.

## Open questions

- Product layers — how many sit between HoP and CEO? Is there a CPO or COO above?
- Direct-reports composition — PM ICs, or mostly team leads and squad leads?
- IPO / exit timeline — and what that means for equity value joining now?
