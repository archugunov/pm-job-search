# Research Brief — Stripe

## Company snapshot

- **Stage:** Late-stage payments infrastructure, large org.
- **What they sell:** APIs for accepting money, managing subscriptions, handling payouts. Merchant-facing.
- **The role:** Lead PM, Growth — owned merchant activation funnels (signup → first successful transaction). Core metric: time-to-first-charge. Levers: onboarding UX, docs, integration support.

## Why this fits

**P1 on the rubric, P2 on honest read. Rejected at take-home.**

- **Brand pull:** Top-tier fintech brand and the role is senior enough — that's what scored it P1.
- **Domain mismatch:** My positioning is consumer credit and lending. Stripe Growth is B2B merchant activation. Adjacent skill set, not the same problem.
- **Take-home signal:** Designed metrics for merchant behaviour, not consumer financial journeys. The panel could tell the difference.
- **Rejection note:** "Going with a candidate with more direct payments experience" — confirmed rather than surprised me.
- **Re-engagement criterion:** Keep Stripe on monitoring. A Consumer Credit or Lending product role would flip the fit entirely — that's the version where positioning lands rather than requires translation.

## Open questions

- Consumer credit / lending product on the roadmap that would be a closer fit?
- Re-application timeline — is Stripe effectively closed for now, or is there a role / window to watch?
- Take-home weight — how much was payments-domain experience vs general product-growth skill? Would a stronger framing have closed the gap?
