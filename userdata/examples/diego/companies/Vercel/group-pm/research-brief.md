## Company snapshot

The DX Group PM role owns the developer experience surface end-to-end across Vercel — onboarding flows, CLI tooling, deployment workflows, and the integrations layer (GitHub/GitLab/Bitbucket sync, framework integrations for Next.js, Nuxt, SvelteKit, etc.). The Group PM manages five PMs across those sub-surfaces. Scope is Vercel-wide, with cross-functional dependencies into infra, edge, and enterprise go-to-market. DX in this context means the full pre- and post-deploy loop from the developer's perspective: how fast can you go from repo to production, how clear is the feedback loop when something breaks, how smooth is the upgrade path as a team grows from self-serve to enterprise. The team interacts with both PLG and enterprise motions because the DX surface is the funnel entry point for both.

## Why this fits

Group PM scope with five PMs and ownership of the full DX surface is real product leadership — not IC with a different title. Per the scaler-archetype framing I've been using, this is the step the current positioning supports: ready to scale from senior IC into a small team lead in a product-native org. PLG is the monetisation engine here, not an add-on, which keeps the product strategy honest.

Compensation: Group PM at this stage is likely in the $230–280K band, which is above my stated $190–230K ceiling, but the scope and brand make it worth pursuing and negotiating from. Willing to move if the rest is right.

Risk to flag: managing five PMs directly is a step up from prior Senior PM IC work. In the HM round, anchor on hiring and feedback experience — concrete examples of growing PMs or running hiring loops — to address the scope gap proactively. Treating as P0, applied first ahead of the Edge role because the scope + brand + PLG-fit combination doesn't come up often.
