## Company snapshot

Vercel is a frontend cloud + edge compute platform — they own Next.js and have built the dominant deployment layer around it. Around 400 people total, but the Edge Platform team sits at roughly 25. The Edge Platform PM owns the runtime and DX for edge functions: server-side rendering at the edge, middleware execution, edge config, and route caching. The product surface is developer-native — CLI + dashboard parity, GitHub/GitLab deploy integrations, and the edge runtime itself. Monetisation runs on a self-serve growth motion at the team/seat tier; enterprise sales is a layer above, which keeps the PLG motion real rather than cosmetic. The company is still founder-influenced at the product level despite the headcount, which tends to mean faster decisions and less process debt per team.

## Why this fits

The PLG motion here is the core monetisation engine, not a bolted-on acquisition layer — that's the cleanest match against the anti-goal I've been tracking. Edge Platform is small enough (~25) that it still feels founder-led inside a 400-person company, which is the stage-fit sweet spot. Compensation per Levels.fyi is likely within the $190–230K band, so no negotiation gymnastics needed. Remote-OK culture covers the Mexico City setup without needing special carve-outs.

Risk worth flagging: 400 people total can still mean matrix-heavy cross-team dependencies even when the team is small. If this lands an HM round, the question to ask is about RACI and cross-team decision rights — specifically who owns the edge runtime roadmap vs. the infra org. Treating as P0 because the role-shape + PLG-real combination is genuinely rare in the current market.
