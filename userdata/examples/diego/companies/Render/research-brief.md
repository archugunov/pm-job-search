## What they do

Render is a Heroku-style PaaS for modern web app deployment — infrastructure abstracted away, deploy from Git, managed databases, background workers. ~100 ppl, Series C-equivalent. PLG-real with a free tier and clear paid-tier conversion path. Group PM Cloud owns the deployment surface: how apps get built, deployed, and scaled on the platform.

## Why it wasn't a fit

Second take-home rejection in 10 days, same underlying pattern as Fly.io. Render's take-home asked for an architectural deep-dive — specifically how I'd think through the Cloud product's infrastructure surface evolution. I wrote a product-strategy doc. Their feedback named the mismatch directly: "the take-home asked for an architectural deep-dive your submission framed as a product strategy doc."

This is the second data point. Both Fly.io and Render have infra-shaped take-homes where the rubric weights technical and architectural depth. My default PM framing is strategic and customer-outcome-led. That's a real gap when the role is infrastructure-PM in nature, not apps-PM. The pattern is starting to be visible — need to name it explicitly and adjust before the next take-home lands.
