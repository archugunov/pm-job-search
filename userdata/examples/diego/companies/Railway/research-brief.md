## What they do

Railway is a modern deployment platform — clean self-serve UX, fast onboarding, strong developer brand. ~30 ppl, Series A, founder-led. Strong PLG motion: free tier converts to paid on usage. Senior PM Developer Experience owns the onboarding, CLI, and developer-facing surfaces.

## Why it wasn't a fit

Third same-stage take-home rejection in a row. Fly.io (2026-04-08), Render (2026-04-18), now Railway (2026-04-29) — all rejected at take-home, all with the same signal: "strategic framing, not enough technical depth."

The pattern is clear and I'm naming it: take-home rubrics at dev-tools-infra companies weight technical depth higher than my default PM framing delivers. My submissions are strong on strategy, customer outcomes, and product positioning — but the interviewers at this tier of infra company are looking for candidates who lead with the technical layer first and wrap strategy around it.

Lessons: when the next take-home lands (or I spot the take-home format in prep), shift the framing. Open with the technical architecture or infra tradeoffs, then layer the product strategy on top. Also: a career-coach conversation on this pattern is overdue.
