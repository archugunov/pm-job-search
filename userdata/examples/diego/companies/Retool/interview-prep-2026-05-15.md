## Stories to land

### 1. PLG pricing restructure
**Angle:** "How do you measure PLG conversion at the cohort level?"

At a Series C B2B SaaS I owned the pricing surface end-to-end — packaging, limits, in-app upgrade flows. Paid-conversion had stalled at 4.8% of weekly-active free workspaces. I ran a cohort comparison isolating pricing signal from underlying growth: new-customer cohorts under old vs new pricing, with the analyst building the attribution model jointly. Paid-conversion lifted to 7.2% within four months, and the methodology — not the outcome — is what leadership still references when they talk about instrumentation. For Retool: the Apps team is the PLG motion. The panel will want to see I can instrument it at cohort level, not just report headline conversion.

### 2. DevTools onboarding — zero to one
**Angle:** "0-to-1 product launches in developer tools."

At a public DevTools company I launched the partner program from zero — no prior integrations strategy, no partner data model, no ICP clarity. Spent the first six weeks doing nothing but customer and partner discovery, then wrote a two-page spec on what "integration success" would mean at 12 months. Shipped the first three integrations in a single quarter with one engineer and one technical writer. By year three, annualised contribution was $8M. For Retool: the panel includes an eng manager and design lead. The story demonstrates I can run 0-to-1 in a technically opinionated environment without slowing the engineering team down.

### 3. API deprecation — stakeholder dissent
**Angle:** "Stakeholder management at platform scale."

Owning the API products at a public DevTools company, I had to deprecate a v1 endpoint that ~200 external integrations depended on. The eng team wanted a hard cutoff; enterprise accounts and a vocal subset of internal sales wanted indefinite support. I ran a structured migration: 90-day notice, a migration guide the eng team wrote jointly with me, and a partner call for the top 20 affected integrations. Shipped the cutoff on schedule with zero churn from named accounts. For Retool: the panel will likely probe how I operate across CTO, eng, and design simultaneously — this story shows I can hold a position under dissent and land it without relationship damage.

### 4. Pricing-page experiment — the bet that paid off
**Angle:** "High-conviction pricing bet that paid off."

Late in the pricing-redesign work I pushed for a pricing-page restructure the CEO was uncertain about — it surfaced the seat-based model more prominently than the usage-based framing the product had always led with. The design team had concerns about page hierarchy. I made the call based on two months of cohort data and one unambiguous customer-interview signal: price predictability was the conversion blocker, not product awareness. Outcome: 2.4pp additional paid-conversion lift in the first cohort. For Retool: panels at this stage often test whether the candidate can defend a conviction in the room, not just describe processes. This story is for that moment.
