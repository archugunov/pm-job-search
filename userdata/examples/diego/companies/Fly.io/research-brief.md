## What they do

Fly.io is a global edge runtime — deploy apps close to users across dozens of regions, with per-region container orchestration. Series A, ~60 ppl, founder-led. HoP owns the runtime and DX product surface end-to-end: how developers deploy, debug, and scale apps on the platform.

## Why it wasn't a fit

The take-home revealed the gap clearly. The assignment was an infrastructure-PM strategy doc — thinking through runtime architecture tradeoffs, regional failover, and the ops primitives that make Fly different from competing platforms. My submission was strong on product strategy but light on the infra-depth the rubric was weighting. Their feedback confirmed it: "prioritising a candidate with prior infrastructure-PM experience."

In hindsight this should have been scored P2. The HoP role at Fly is infra-shaped, not apps-PLG-shaped. I applied at P1 because the team size and founder-distance looked right on paper — the role spec undersold the infra orientation.

Monitoring: true. If Fly opens an Apps or DX-product role that's less runtime-architecture-heavy, worth a second look. The team quality and culture are strong.
