# PLG pricing restructure

## Situation

Series C B2B SaaS, $26M ARR, usage-based pricing on top of a freemium tier.
Two problems by Q1 2024: paid-conversion had stalled at 4.8% of weekly-active
free workspaces, and net revenue retention on the small-business segment was
below 100% — customers were downgrading rather than expanding. The CEO
wanted "fix the freemium → paid funnel" but the underlying issue was the
pricing surface itself.

## Task

I was VP Product. My remit: own the pricing surface end-to-end — packaging,
limits, in-app upgrade flows, communication. Six months to ship a redesign
that lifted paid-conversion without breaking the PLG motion. Cross-functional
team: one designer, two engineers, one analyst (loaned from finance).

## Action

- Spent the first three weeks in customer calls. Twenty conversations with
  downgraded SMB customers; the pattern was clear — pricing tiers didn't map
  to the way teams actually used the product. They wanted seat-based
  predictability, not usage-based exposure.
- Co-designed three pricing-architecture options with the analyst and the
  CFO. Modelled revenue impact, churn risk, and migration complexity for each.
  Ran them by ten existing customers under NDA before picking the winner.
- Shipped the new pricing in three phases over four months: new customers
  first (test the conversion lift), then opt-in migration for existing
  customers (test the churn risk), then forced migration for the long tail
  (with grandfathered pricing for the top 50 by ARR).
- Owned the in-app upgrade flow redesign with the designer — three iterations
  in two months, each tested with weekly cohort analyses.

## Result

- Paid-conversion lifted from 4.8% to 7.2% on new free signups within four
  months of the new pricing going live to new customers.
- ARR grew from $26M to $40M over 14 months. Pricing-redesign attribution
  (via cohort comparison) was responsible for roughly $5-6M of that lift —
  the rest from organic growth and other product work.
- SMB net revenue retention recovered to 108% by month nine.
- No measurable churn lift on the migrated cohorts; one large customer
  negotiated a custom rate, the rest accepted the new pricing.

## Angles for different prompts

**"Tell me about a 0-to-1."** Lead with the *pricing architecture* as the
0-to-1, not the conversion lift. The thing that didn't exist was the
seat-based packaging.

**"Tell me about working with the CEO / founder."** Lead with the reframing
— the CEO asked for funnel fix, I came back with pricing-architecture work.
The story is how I got him to that conclusion, not the analytics.

**"Tell me about analytics depth."** Lead with the cohort comparison
methodology — comparing new-customer cohorts under old vs new pricing,
isolating the attribution. Avoid the usual "we A/B tested" framing.

**"Tell me about a failure."** Lead with the first packaging option I
championed — it failed customer NDA review at week six, I had to scrap and
restart. Cost a month. What I learned about testing pricing with real
customers before architecture lock.
