## Company snapshot

Motor insurance for non-standard segments — immigrants, expats, and new UK arrivals who lack local credit history and get priced out or rejected by mainstream insurers. Series B, ~100 ppl, founder-led, London-based. The Pricing & Risk PM owns the rating model and portfolio-risk balancing: how do you price a driver with no UK history fairly without bleeding on adverse selection?

## Why this fits

Strong P0. The skill transfer from consumer-credit risk to insurance pricing is one domain hop, not a career pivot. Both are the same core problem: price a population where standard signals are weak or missing, manage default/loss rates, and keep unit economics green while growing the book. My consumer-credit risk modelling is half the work; I would learn the underwriting nuances on top of that.

Stage-fit is excellent: 100 ppl is inside my preferred 80-150 band, founder-led, London-based. Compensation likely lands at £130-150K which is inside my range.

Honest risk: insurance domain is new. I have never owned an underwriting rating model directly. Mitigation is that the PM role here is not actuary-level quant work — it is translating risk insight into product and pricing decisions, which is exactly what I do in credit.
