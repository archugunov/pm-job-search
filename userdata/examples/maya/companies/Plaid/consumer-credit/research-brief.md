# Plaid — Senior PM, Consumer Credit

## What they do

Plaid is known as fintech infrastructure — the bank-connection APIs that power
half the apps in your wallet — but Consumer Credit is a distinct, founder-driven
bet to move the company from B2B rails into direct-to-consumer credit products.
The team is about 18 people inside a ~280-person parent. They own the full
consumer journey: onboarding, first-loan UX, and the risk-pricing trade-offs
that sit underneath. The role is less "PM on an established product" and more
"define what this thing should be as it finds product-market fit." They've got
data and infrastructure advantages that a standalone lender would kill for —
connectivity to bank rails, income verification, real-time account data — but
the consumer-product muscle is still being built.

One flag worth raising before I accept any offer: pricing decisions get gated
by the Capital Markets team, not by the Consumer Credit PM. That handoff could
be friction or could be a non-issue depending on how embedded the PM is in that
process. I need to ask about it directly in the CPO round.

## Why it's a fit

My positioning is the messy middle — post-initial-credit, pre-prime, thin-file
users who conventional lenders drop. That is exactly the customer segment Plaid
Consumer Credit is targeting. The team-size headline (18) feels small but the
parent company gives you compliance, infrastructure, and distribution that a
true Series A wouldn't have — I've worked in that structure and it's the best
of both shapes. The founder-driven nature of the Consumer Credit bet means I'd
report close to the CEO, not through a five-layer product hierarchy. That's the
autonomy profile I'm optimising for. London base, hybrid 2 days a week from
the Shoreditch office — clean fit with my hybrid-only constraint.
