# Debrief — Plaid Consumer Credit panel (2026-05-12)

## What landed

- The pricing-experiment story landed deeply — I took it from "we ran a test" to "we designed a system for testing under capital constraints", and the CTO followed up with three pushes on the methodology. Why it worked: the framing matched their actual operating environment (constrained pricing decisions, infrastructure advantages, capital allocation tradeoffs).
- The question I asked about how Consumer Credit interfaces with the parent's data infrastructure got a genuine, detailed answer from the Senior PM. Why it worked: it signalled I'd done the research and wasn't treating this as a generic PM role.

## What didn't

- The activation framework discussion never got airtime. I had a prepared response about onboarding-to-first-loan conversion benchmarks and it never came up — almost no time was spent on the top-of-funnel. Why it missed: the panel was skewed toward risk and pricing depth, not growth mechanics, and I misjudged the emphasis in my prep.
- I gave a slightly hedged answer on "how do you operate when you don't control the pricing lever?" which felt unresolved. Why it missed: I didn't have a concrete example ready — I reframed the question instead of answering it directly.

## Interviewer signals

- CTO drove the depth; seemed to be testing technical PM credibility, not just product instinct. Pushed on pricing methodology twice and nodded when I named specific statistical concepts.
- Senior PM peer was quieter — asked one question about cross-functional process and seemed satisfied with a short answer. Possibly assessing culture fit rather than domain depth.
- The panel composition (CTO + Senior PM peer, plus presumably one recruiter) suggests this round is primarily a technical credibility gate.

## Deltas vs prep doc

- I'd swap the activation-framework angle out of my top-three for CPO prep — it didn't land here and won't be the CPO's primary probe either. Replace with something that shows strategic positioning under ambiguity.
- The anchor point about team-autonomy-inside-parent infrastructure was never explicitly raised but was implicitly present in every question. It's a live concern for the CPO round — worth naming proactively rather than waiting for them to surface it.
