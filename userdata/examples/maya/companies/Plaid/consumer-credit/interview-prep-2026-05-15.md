## Stories to land

### 1. Payments pricing experiment
**Angle:** "Pricing as a system, not just a test — how does a pricing decision interact with capital allocation and downstream default exposure?"

Led the framing of a pricing experiment as a capital-allocation question, not a conversion-rate question. The situation was that pricing signals were being read in isolation from cohort-quality — we were optimising for revenue lift without accounting for the adverse-selection loop. I reframed the problem to include the default-exposure dimension, built a joint model with Risk, and ran the test with explicit NPL-cap guardrails. The result was a 2.7pp improvement in net margin, not just gross conversion. For Plaid CC: Tom-equivalent will probe whether I understand the upstream/downstream of pricing decisions. Framing it as a capital-allocation system lands better than framing it as "I ran A/B tests" — it matches the operating environment he's built.

### 2. Consumer credit onboarding pivot
**Angle:** "Strategy revision mid-flight, not just execution — how do you hold a position under founder pressure?"

Twelve weeks into an onboarding redesign, user behaviour data contradicted the founding assumption about where drop-off was happening. The HM was pushing to stay the course; I presented a recommendation to pivot the funnel entry point using a 3-slide deck anchored on unit economics, not user sentiment. We pivoted. First-draw conversion improved by 1.9pp within 60 days. For Plaid CC: the CPO will probe product ownership and whether I can hold a strategic position under founder pressure — this is the story that answers that directly.

### 3. Risk team dissent
**Angle:** "When I held a position against a stronger team — and why that was the right move."

Risk team blocked a feature launch because of a marginal NPL-exposure concern. I had the data showing the exposure was within the agreed risk envelope; they had institutional authority. I built a shared-assumptions document, ran a joint sensitivity analysis, and made the case in writing to the CPO. The feature launched with an NPL tripwire monitor added. Why it held: I named the disagreement cleanly, gave them a clear decision structure, and didn't route around them. For Plaid CC: Capital Markets gates pricing decisions — dissent management is a live skill I'll need from day one.

### 4. Underwriting zero-to-one
**Angle:** "Building new capability inside an existing infrastructure — how do you move when you don't control the rails?"

Launched an underwriting product from scratch inside a parent infrastructure that wasn't built for it — API latency constraints, data-sharing limits, and a vendor contract that required renegotiation. Got to first decision in 11 weeks by scoping the MVP tightly, surfacing the infrastructure constraints as product decisions (not just engineering backlog), and running parallel vendor negotiation. For Plaid CC: the Consumer Credit division is a 0-to-1 product inside a B2B infrastructure parent — this mirrors the constraint exactly and shows I've navigated it before.
