# Interview prep — Plaid Consumer Credit cpo-round (2026-05-15)

## Stories to lead with

1. **payments-pricing-experiment** — angle: pricing as a system (not just a test). Why for this round: Tom-equivalent will push on whether I understand the upstream/downstream of pricing decisions; framing it as a capital-allocation system lands better than framing it as "I ran A/B tests".
2. **consumer-credit-onboarding-pivot** — angle: strategy revision mid-flight, not just execution. Why for this round: the CPO will probe product ownership and whether I can hold a strategic position under founder pressure.
3. **risk-team-dissent** — angle: when I held a position against a stronger team and why. Why for this round: with Capital Markets gating pricing decisions, dissent management will be a live skill — I should demonstrate I've navigated it before.
4. **underwriting-zero-to-one** — angle: building new capability inside an existing infrastructure. Why for this round: Plaid CC is a 0-to-1 product inside a B2B infrastructure parent — this mirrors the constraint exactly.

## Anchor points from research

- Capital Markets gating: pricing decisions require Capital Markets sign-off, not just PM discretion. I need to ask directly how embedded the Consumer Credit PM is in that process — framing it as "I want to understand the operating model" rather than as a complaint.
- Team size 18: small team inside ~280-person parent. The mandate is PMF-finding, not optimisation. I should calibrate stories toward founder-speed contexts, not scaled-product contexts.
- Founder-driven consumer credit bet: the Consumer Credit division is a deliberate strategic bet, not a spin-off or acqui-hire — which means internal selling is still live and the CPO round likely includes an implicit culture-fit filter.

## Questions I want to ask

- How embedded is the Consumer Credit PM in the Capital Markets process day-to-day — observer, input, or voting participant?
- What does a typical week look like — product vs meetings?
- Where has the last HoP spent most political capital internally?
- When something underperforms — who's in the debrief room?

## Things to NOT do

- Don't frame pricing experience as "I drove revenue" — frame it as "I understood the system and made a defensible bet". The CPO will probe depth, not outcomes.
- Don't undersell the Capital Markets question — if I don't raise it I look either naive or avoidant. Raise it early in Q&A, not as an afterthought.
- Don't lead with team size managed — there's likely no direct-report scope at this stage; lead with outcome and autonomy instead.
