# Plaid — Senior PM, Growth Loops

## What they do

The Growth Loops PM at Plaid owns the referral and viral mechanisms that sit
across both the developer (API) side and the consumer product side. In practice
that means: how does Plaid get developers to embed its products, share them,
and expand usage organically; and on the consumer side, how does credit-product
usage compound through referral or social proof. It's a PLG role at a company
that is still figuring out whether PLG even applies to its consumer line. The
Plaid brand gives the role a strong platform, and the data infrastructure
advantage means the PM can run experiments that competitors can't — but the
growth surface spans two very different user types (developers and consumers),
which makes focus hard.

## Why it's a fit

Honest answer: it's a softer fit than Consumer Credit. My positioning is
consumer-credit, thin-file, messy-middle — not developer growth or PLG. I
understand referral mechanics and growth loops in general, but I'd be stepping
away from my core positioning rather than doubling down on it. I'd take this if
Consumer Credit doesn't land. The Plaid brand still matters for the CV, and the
team-size and autonomy shape are similar to the CC role. Treating it as a
backup — P1, not P0 — and I'll apply softly once the CC outcome is clear rather
than running a parallel process that splits my prep.
