## What they do

Consumer credit lender, UK-origin, expanding into secured products. Raised a £130M growth round late 2025 — effectively Series C stage. ~120 ppl across product, eng, risk, and ops. Two-sided business: borrower acquisition on one side, capital markets funding partners on the other. Personal loans are the core, with a secured credit line in build. The HoP role owns the consumer credit surface end-to-end as the company moves toward IPO readiness.

## Why it's a fit

This is the clearest shape match in the pipeline. Consumer Credit HoP is messy-middle work by definition — pricing experiments, retention cohorts, default rate monitoring, loan-level unit economics. That is exactly what I do. Stage-fit: 120 ppl sits right in my preferred 80-150 band, and the CEO is still in product debates, which is what I want.

Compensation overlaps cleanly: Levels.fyi data points to £140-165K base with 0.1-0.3% equity range.

Risks: Tom has strong product opinions himself and I asked the political-capital question in the CPO round. His answer was specific and unhedged, which is a good sign. The remaining wildcard is IPO-path equity dilution — will read the term sheet carefully before signing.
