# Debrief — Lendable panel (2026-05-02)

## What landed

- The framing that pricing, retention, and default loops are one system landed cleanly — I introduced it explicitly at the start and the CTO referenced it back to me later in the session. Why it worked: the panel included Head of Risk and CTO, both of whom think in system terms; naming the system first gave them a shared frame for the rest of the session.
- The failed-launch story (instalments-failed-launch) landed better than expected — Head of Risk asked a follow-up about how the postmortem changed my launch checklist, and I had a concrete answer. Why it worked: showing that failure produced a specific process change, not just a lesson learned, is credible to a risk-oriented interviewer.
- The risk-team-dissent story gave Head of Risk a chance to probe my understanding of her function. She seemed satisfied — asked "what would you have done differently" and I had a direct answer. Why it worked: I came in with genuine respect for risk constraints, not a PM-vs-risk framing.

## What didn't

- Missed naming a specific number for the activation-to-first-loan funnel. I mentioned "below-benchmark conversion" and moved on without quantifying — the CTO's body language tightened. Why it missed: I had the number (4.1% signup-to-first-draw on a 90-day window) and didn't use it. Prep note said to name it; I didn't under pressure.
- The capital-markets-gating question I planned to raise didn't come up from them — I asked it in Q&A and got a short, guarded answer from CTO ("that's a Tom conversation"). Why it missed: asking a structural/political question to the wrong person flags naivety. Save the capital-markets question for Tom's round.

## Interviewer signals

- CTO surfaced the Capital Markets gating concern unprompted at one point — he mentioned that "pricing decisions have two layers of approval at Lendable" without elaborating. That's a live topic inside the company, not just on my research brief.
- Head of Risk asked zero process questions and all substantive ones — she's not checking PM hygiene, she's checking whether I understand risk as a product constraint.
- Senior PM peer barely spoke — asked one question about roadmap prioritisation and seemed comfortable. Likely the culture-fit observer in this round.
