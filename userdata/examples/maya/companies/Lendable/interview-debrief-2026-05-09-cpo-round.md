# Debrief — Lendable cpo-round (2026-05-09)

## What landed

- The founder-vetting questions landed well — all three were asked and answered, and Tom's responses were specific and unhedged. The political-capital question ("where has the last HoP spent most political capital internally?") got the best answer: Tom named the capital markets / product interface as the primary friction point, said the previous HoP spent too long trying to negotiate around it rather than through it, and that he wanted a HoP who would "own the conversation, not avoid it". Why it worked: asking the question framed me as someone who does due diligence on founders, not just companies — and his answer was a genuine signal, not a rehearsed one.
- The weekly-rhythm question ("what does a typical week look like — product vs meetings?") revealed that Tom spends roughly 40% of his week in product reviews, which is more than I expected. Why it worked: it surfaced a real expectation about CEO involvement that I can make a decision with.
- The pricing-experiment story at the system level held up under Tom's probing — he pushed on the Capital Markets handoff specifically and I was ready for it. Why it worked: I had read the CTO's signal from the panel debrief and prepared the Capital Markets framing explicitly.

## What didn't

- The "if it fails in 18 months, what's the story?" question went too theoretical. I described a category of failure (poor unit economics eroding the capital markets relationship) without naming a concrete scenario and concrete exit narrative. Why it missed: Tom wanted a specific story, not a taxonomy. The answer needed to be "if the NPL rate climbs above X and the capital markets partner pulls the facility, I'd have been the person who argued for growth velocity over credit quality — and I'd need to own that call."
- The "when something underperforms — who's in the debrief room?" question got a good answer from Tom but I didn't follow up with a clarifying question I'd planned: "has that ever included you walking something back publicly?" Missed an opportunity to probe how Tom handles being wrong.

## Interviewer signals

- Tom's political-capital answer was unhedged and specific — this is unusual for a founder at this stage. Either he's a straight talker or the debrief is part of the process. Assume the former; verify through reference check.
- His engagement was highest during the founder-vetting portion — he seemed to enjoy the mutual due diligence framing. That's a good cultural read.
- No recruiter present; just Tom and me. That's a standard CPO-equivalent format at founder-led companies at this stage — confirms this was a real final-gate round.

## Deltas vs prep doc

- The 18-month-downside question came up almost verbatim; I wasn't sharp enough. Add a prepared 18-month-downside story to the standard CPO-round prep template for all future companies.
- The follow-up probe on "when has Tom walked something back publicly" is a question I'll carry into reference checks — it's important for assessing ego and learning speed.
