# Interview prep — Lendable panel (2026-04-30)

## Stories to lead with

1. **payments-pricing-experiment** — angle: pricing/retention/default loops as one interconnected system, not three separate workstreams. Why for this round: the panel includes Head of Risk and CTO — framing the pricing work as a system that accounts for capital costs and default exposure will resonate more than framing it as a growth intervention.
2. **consumer-credit-onboarding-pivot** — angle: mid-flight strategy revision driven by unit-economics signals. Why for this round: Lendable is moving toward IPO readiness, which means the panel will be assessing whether I can make calls under commercial pressure, not just in stable conditions.
3. **risk-team-dissent** — angle: building cross-functional trust while holding a position the risk team initially opposed. Why for this round: with Head of Risk in the room, I want to demonstrate I understand risk constraints and can work alongside them, not just push back.
4. **instalments-failed-launch** — angle: what I learned from a launch that didn't work and how I course-corrected. Why for this round: IPO-track companies do reference-check post-mortems and the panel may probe for failure modes — showing I've named mine and fixed them is better than having none.

## Anchor points from research

- Capital Markets gating: Lendable's two-sided model means product decisions have a funding-partner side as well as a borrower side. The CTO may probe how I'd think about a feature that benefits borrowers but tightens the capital cost structure — I need an answer for that trade-off.
- 120 ppl / IPO-track: the role is Head of Product, not Senior PM. The panel will be assessing whether I can step up to cross-squad coordination and strategic roadmap ownership, not just individual delivery.
- CEO still in product debates: Tom's involvement level from the HM round suggests he'll have strong opinions in the room even when he's not present — the panel may reflect his frameworks and priorities. Worth asking the panel what Tom cares about most in the next 12 months.

## Questions I want to ask

- What does the Capital Markets team need to see before a pricing decision gets greenlit — and how much advance time does the PM typically have?
- What's the biggest thing Tom would say the product team hasn't solved yet?
- How does the Head of Risk role interface with the product roadmap — input at spec stage, or veto at launch?

## Things to NOT do

- Don't soft-pedal the failed-launch story if it comes up — panels at IPO-track companies have seen enough to spot a sanitised postmortem. Name the failure, name my part in it, name what changed.
- Don't let the Head of Risk interview feel adversarial — I should be the person in the room who most understands their constraints, not the one defending against them.
- Don't neglect to name a specific number for the activation-to-first-loan funnel if it comes up — I have the data, I should use it.
