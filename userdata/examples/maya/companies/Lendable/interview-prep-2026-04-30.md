## Stories to land

### 1. Payments pricing experiment
**Angle:** "Pricing/retention/default loops as one interconnected system — not three separate workstreams."

Reframed a pricing experiment to treat capital cost, conversion rate, and default exposure as a single system rather than three sequential handoffs. Built the model jointly with Risk and ran the test with NPL guardrails explicit in the brief. Result: 2.7pp net margin improvement, with the Risk team co-owning the outcome rather than reviewing it after the fact. For Lendable: the panel includes Head of Risk and CTO. Framing the pricing work as a system that accounts for capital costs and default exposure will resonate more than framing it as a growth intervention — it matches how both of those functions think.

### 2. Consumer credit onboarding pivot
**Angle:** "Mid-flight strategy revision driven by unit-economics signals — not just user feedback."

Twelve weeks into an onboarding redesign, unit-economics data showed the new flow was improving conversion but degrading first-draw-to-repayment behaviour. Made the call to reorient the funnel entry point around borrower intent signals rather than application completion speed. For Lendable: the company is moving toward IPO readiness, which means the panel will be assessing whether I can make calls under commercial pressure, not just in stable conditions. This story answers that.

### 3. Risk team dissent
**Angle:** "Building cross-functional trust while holding a position the risk team initially opposed."

Risk team blocked a feature launch because of a marginal NPL-exposure concern that was within the agreed risk envelope. Rather than escalating, I built a shared-assumptions document, ran a joint sensitivity analysis, and made the case in writing. We launched with an NPL tripwire monitor added — which Risk proposed. For Lendable: with Head of Risk in the room, I want to demonstrate I understand risk constraints and can work alongside them, not just push back. The story shows I treat Risk as a design input, not a sign-off gate.

### 4. Instalments failed launch
**Angle:** "What I learned from a launch that didn't work — and the specific process change that resulted."

Launched an instalment product variant that didn't find traction. The failure was a misjudgement of user intent at the top of the funnel — we built for the user who had already decided to use instalments, not the user who was considering it. Postmortem produced a specific change: we now run a 2-week intent-validation sprint before any funnel redesign. For Lendable: IPO-track companies do reference-check post-mortems and the panel may probe for failure modes. Showing I've named mine and fixed them is better than having none.

## Anti-patterns to avoid this round
- Don't soft-pedal the failed-launch story if it comes up — panels at IPO-track companies have seen enough to spot a sanitised postmortem. Name the failure, name my part in it, name what changed.
- Don't let the Head of Risk interview feel adversarial — I should be the person in the room who most understands their constraints, not the one defending against them.
- Don't neglect to name a specific number for the activation-to-first-loan funnel if it comes up — I have the data (4.1% signup-to-first-draw on a 90-day window), I should use it.
