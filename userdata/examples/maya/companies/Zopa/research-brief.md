## What they do

UK digital bank, consumer credit roots. Started as a P2P lender in 2005, transitioned into a full retail bank with a banking licence. ~600 ppl, profitable, on an IPO track. The Consumer Credit PM role owns the credit product surface — personal loans, credit cards, risk-pricing — and sits inside a larger product org with multiple layers.

## Why it's a fit (or wasn't)

Product surface is a strong fit. Consumer credit is what I do, and Zopa's credit book is mature enough that the PM role involves real pricing and risk decisions, not just feature work.

The problem is size. 600 ppl is way over my preferred 80-150 ceiling. At that scale the PM role will have more matrix dependencies, more approval layers, and less direct founder-distance than I want. That is why this is P1 and sitting in to_apply rather than already submitted.

The plan: hold off on applying until the Lendable outcome is clear. If Lendable lands, Zopa is irrelevant. If Lendable falls through, Zopa is a credible parallel option because the surface fit is genuine — I just have to accept the size trade-off consciously.
