# Interview prep — Mercury / Sr PM, Banking
**Date:** 2026-05-11  **Stage:** hm  **Interviewer:** Sarah Chen (Head of Product, Banking)  **Tier:** P1

## Anchors

- **Why this company:** Mercury Banking surface is mid-rebuild — the team owning it is 8 people inside a 700-person org, so day-to-day stage feel is closer to a 50-person co. That's the structural fit, even if the wrapper is bigger than I'd usually target.
- **Why this role:** Sr PM (not Head of) — narrower scope than my Lendable trajectory, but the rebuild project is meaty product work, not stakeholder management dressed up.
- **Salary floor:** £130K base. Mercury's RSUs are meaningful-but-illiquid; treat them as upside, not part of the base.
- **The gap to name:** I haven't shipped deposit-product mechanics or treasury workflows before. Frame as a 3-month onboarding curve, not a ceiling.

## Stories to land

### 1. Onboarding pivot (lead with this one)
**Angle:** "Mid-flight strategy revision driven by unit-economics signals — not just user feedback."

The instinct from Mercury's research-brief: the Banking org is being judged on activation-through-month-3 retention. That's the same shape as the onboarding pivot at my last role. Lead with this story because the company-shape match is direct.

### 2. Risk team dissent
**Angle:** "Treating Risk and Compliance as a design input, not a sign-off gate."

Mercury operates inside BSA/KYC/AML; any product change touches Compliance. Sarah's last shipped initiative (per her LinkedIn — Q4 2025) was a re-onboarding flow that cleared CDD review in a single sprint. Story signals I work the same way she does.

## Questions to ask Sarah

- What's the realistic legacy-core migration position by end of year? What does "done" actually look like — and how is the team measuring it?
- You came in ~14 months ago and the Banking org was reshaped soon after. What did the shape look like before, and what triggered the rework?
- How autonomous is the Banking PM on fee-structure decisions — what gets escalated to the CPO or CFO?
- What's the biggest thing the Banking team hasn't solved yet that you wish they had?

## Anti-patterns to avoid

- Don't overclaim banking-domain depth. If she probes deposit mechanics, say "I haven't shipped this — here's my 3-month plan to close the gap" and move on. Pretending is the worst answer.
- Don't lead with credit/lending stories. Pricing-experiment is a fit but only on second pass — banking interviewers tune out lending stories fast.
- Don't pitch the role as "interim to Head of Product." Sarah filled this role to keep it filled. The "promotion in 18 months" framing reads as not-here-for-the-job.
