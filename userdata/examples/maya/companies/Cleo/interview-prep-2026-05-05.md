# Interview prep — Cleo hm (2026-05-05)

## Stories to lead with

1. **payments-pricing-experiment** — angle: activation and growth motion (how the pricing experiment fed into the retention funnel, not just the initial conversion). Why for this round: the HM will care about growth mechanics and funnel thinking — leading with the growth dimension of a pricing story bridges my background to their surface.
2. **consumer-credit-onboarding-pivot** — angle: onboarding-funnel redesign under ambiguous user intent. Why for this round: Cleo's activation funnel is chat-first and has the same thin-signal problem as credit onboarding — demonstrating that I've navigated ambiguous activation problems lands better than generic activation frameworks.
3. **underwriting-zero-to-one** — angle: building a new user journey where no baseline exists. Why for this round: Cleo is extending its product surface with AI chat — showing I've launched features into a zero-baseline context is relevant to their current build phase.

## Anchor points from research

- AI personal finance growth motion: Cleo's core activation loop is get-user → first money moment → habit formation. My activation-funnel experience maps to the second and third steps — I should frame it in those terms, not in credit-product terms.
- Activation funnels over AI chat: the product surface is chat-first, not dashboard-first. My instinct to reach for funnel visualisation needs to translate into conversational UX framing — ask how they measure "first meaningful money moment" and mirror their language.
- Consumer finance, not consumer credit: Cleo is a finance assistant, not a lender. The positioning distinction matters — users don't come to Cleo to get a loan, they come to understand their money. I need to suppress credit-product framing and lead with the consumer finance angle.

## Questions I want to ask

- How do you currently define "first meaningful money moment" — is it an explicit metric or still being debated?
- What's the biggest funnel drop in the activation flow right now — is it pre-chat or within the first session?
- Where does the Growth PM sit relative to the data science team when they're running activation experiments?

## Things to NOT do

- Don't frame experience through consumer-credit risk — Cleo is a consumer finance assistant, not a lender, and leading with credit-risk depth will read as a domain mismatch.
- Don't use NPL, default rate, or underwriting vocabulary in the first half of the conversation — those are signals that I'm thinking about the wrong product surface.
- Don't bring up Cleo's size as a risk I'm taking on — I already decided to test that boundary; flagging it in the interview reads as ambivalent.
