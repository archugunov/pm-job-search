**Date:** 2026-05-05  **Stage:** hm  **Tier:** P1

## Stories to land

### 1. Payments pricing experiment
**Angle:** "Activation and growth motion — how a pricing experiment fed the retention funnel, not just initial conversion."

Ran a pricing experiment that was nominally about conversion but structurally about retention. The pricing change affected the first-draw experience; users who went through the new flow had meaningfully higher 30-day retention because the product framing at signup matched the actual product they found. The growth insight was that pricing isn't just an acquisition lever — it's an activation signal. For Cleo: the HM will care about growth mechanics and funnel thinking. Leading with the growth dimension of a pricing story bridges my background to their surface without triggering the consumer-credit framing mismatch.

### 2. Consumer credit onboarding pivot
**Angle:** "Onboarding-funnel redesign under ambiguous user intent — when the data tells you the user wanted something different from what you built."

The onboarding was designed for users who already understood the product; the actual users were exploring. Drop-off was highest at a step that assumed too much prior knowledge. I ran a 2-week discovery sprint (5 user interviews, drop-off analysis by cohort), found the assumption was wrong, and redesigned the entry point. For Cleo: the activation funnel is chat-first and has the same thin-signal problem as credit onboarding — users don't tell you what they want, they leave. Demonstrating I've navigated ambiguous activation problems lands better than generic activation frameworks.

### 3. Underwriting zero-to-one
**Angle:** "Building a new user journey where no baseline exists — how do you instrument and iterate without a conversion benchmark?"

Launched an underwriting journey from scratch — no historical baseline, no comparable product to benchmark against. Built a measurement framework first: defined the funnel stages, instrumented each one, set provisional targets from adjacent markets. Got to a meaningful baseline within 6 weeks of launch. For Cleo: the product surface is extending into AI chat, which is also a zero-baseline context. Showing I've launched features without an existing conversion target is more useful than showing I've optimised against an existing one.

## Anti-patterns to avoid this round
- Don't frame experience through consumer-credit risk — Cleo is a consumer finance assistant, not a lender, and leading with credit-risk depth will read as a domain mismatch.
- Don't use NPL, default rate, or underwriting vocabulary in the first half of the conversation — those signal the wrong product surface.
- Don't bring up Cleo's size as a risk I'm taking on — I already decided to test that boundary; flagging it in the interview reads as ambivalent.
- Don't pivot to credit-domain validation when asked about growth — the role is growth-first, and any pivot back reads as a domain-mismatch signal.
